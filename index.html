Linus Torvalds

About Linus Torvalds

Linus Benedict Torvalds (born December 28, 1969) is a Finnish-American software engineer who is the creator and, historically, the principal developer of the Linux kernel, which became the kernel for many Linux distributions and operating systems such as Android and Chrome OS. He also created the distributed version control system Git and the diving logging and planning software Subsurface. He has been honored with various awards for his contributions to computing, including the 2012 Millennium Technology Prize from the Technology Academy Finland.

Torvalds was born in Helsinki, Finland. He is the son of journalists Anna and Nils Torvalds, a member of the European Parliament. He is a Swedish-speaking Finn and holds dual Finnish and American citizenship. He attended the University of Helsinki from 1988 to 1996, graduating with a master's degree in computer science. During this time, he developed an interest in computer operating systems, and worked on several small projects.

In 1991, Torvalds began working on a new operating system kernel, which he initially called "Freax". He later renamed it Linux, a combination of his first name and the word "Unix". The first version of the Linux kernel was released in September 1991, and it quickly gained popularity among programmers and computer enthusiasts. Torvalds continued to develop and improve the kernel, releasing new versions regularly.

In addition to his work on the Linux kernel, Torvalds has also been involved in the development of other software projects. In 2005, he created Git, a distributed version control system that is widely used in software development. He has also worked on Subsurface, a diving logging and planning software.

Torvalds has received numerous awards and honors for his contributions to computing. In 2012, he was awarded the Millennium Technology Prize, which is considered one of the most prestigious awards in the field of technology. He has also been inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame and the Computer History Museum.

Despite his success, Torvalds is known for his outspoken and sometimes controversial personality. He has been criticized for his harsh language and behavior towards other developers, and has been involved in several public disputes. However, he remains a highly respected figure in the tech community, and his contributions to the field of computing have had a significant impact on the industry.

In recent years, Torvalds has focused on improving the Linux kernel and addressing issues related to security and performance. He continues to be an active developer and is involved in the open-source community. Torvalds' work has helped to shape the modern computing landscape, and his contributions will continue to be felt for years to come.
